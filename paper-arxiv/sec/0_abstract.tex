\begin{abstract}
Street-view perception models predict attributes such as safety, yet do
not explain which scene elements should change to shift judgement. We
propose \emph{interventional attribution}: a protocol that tests
structured single-lever edits rather than correlational saliency over
pixels or regions. Each lever specifies a semantic concept, grounded
scene support, edit direction, and constrained prompt template. Edited
candidates are retained only if they pass validity checks for same-place
preservation, locality, realism, and plausibility. In a 50-scene pilot
across five cities, 177 of 250 proposed edits pass this gate and 95 of
those exceed an auxiliary safety threshold. Mobility Infrastructure and
Physical Maintenance are the most promising families in the current
setup. The contribution is the protocol and pilot benchmark, not a
human-validated causal claim: pairwise human evaluation remains the
ground-truth endpoint.
\end{abstract}
