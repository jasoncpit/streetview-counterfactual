\section{Conclusion}

We presented a lever-based interventional attribution framework for street-view perception that reframes scene explainability as a bounded search over structured, theory-grounded counterfactual edits. Rather than treating saliency over pixels or regions as the explanatory endpoint, the framework uses semantically coherent, validity-audited single-lever interventions as the unit of analysis. In a pilot over 50 scenes, this formulation yields a reproducible lower-bound baseline, a practical failure taxonomy, and preliminary proxy-based signals that can guide future human evaluation.

We view the present study as a pilot benchmark rather than a completed human-validated explanation system. Its value lies in formalising the intervention unit, making the generation-and-audit process explicit, and identifying where future work should focus: planner triage, stronger editing control, critic calibration, and pairwise human judgement as the ground-truth endpoint.
