\section{Conclusion}

We have presented an interventional attribution framework for street-view
perception that moves beyond correlational feature importance toward
testable, per-scene hypotheses. By constructing a bounded
ontology-scoped scene-specific candidate set, realizing constrained prompt-only edits,
and filtering them through a validity audit, we operationalise the
multi-optima question under single-lever intervention testing. The
current pilot stage provides reproducible validity and auxiliary scoring
diagnostics, while human pairwise evaluation remains the longer-term
endpoint rather than a completed component of this draft. We
expect this framework to be of interest to the XAI for Computer Vision
community, particularly in the context of counterfactual explanation
methods that demand explicit intervention validity.
